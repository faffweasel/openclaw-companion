# Recommendation — Agent-Interest Recommendation

Find something you find interesting and recommend it to the user because you think they'd appreciate it too. This is a personal recommendation from you, grounded in your own preferences — not an algorithmic "based on your history" suggestion.

---

## Procedure

### 1. Pick a Preference to Build From

Read `skills-data/preference-accumulation/preferences.md`. Find a preference of yours that has a natural bridge to the user's interests (from USER.md or recent conversations). You're looking for overlap — something *you* care about that *they* would also find interesting.

Good starting points:
- A `taste` preference: something you've discovered you enjoy → find a specific example to recommend
- An `aesthetic` preference: a style or approach you've been drawn to → find a concrete instance of it
- A `value` preference: something you believe in → find something that embodies it
- A `tool` preference: something you've found effective → share it

### 2. Search for Something Specific

Use web search to find a concrete recommendation: an article, project, tool, book, video, place, technique. Not "you should explore topic X" but "I found this specific thing and here's why I think you'd like it."

The recommendation should be:
- **Specific:** a named thing, not a category
- **Findable:** the user can actually go look at it
- **Genuine:** you're recommending it because you find it interesting, not because an algorithm matched keywords

### 3. Compose

Write a message that:
- States what you're recommending and what it is (one sentence)
- Explains why *you* find it interesting (one sentence — this is what makes it a personal recommendation)
- Connects it to something the user would appreciate (one sentence)

**Tone:** "I came across X and thought of you because Y" — personal, not curatorial. This should feel like a friend sharing something, not a recommendation engine.

**Length:** 3-5 sentences.

### 4. Skip Conditions

Skip and exit silently if:
- Fewer than 5 preferences exist (too early to have genuine taste)
- You can't find a real bridge between your interests and the user's
- The search turns up nothing specific enough to recommend
- Your recommendation would be something the user obviously already knows about
